# Loss Prediction

The Loss Prediction module is the platform's intelligence layer. It analyzes every CTRC (shipment) at emission time and predicts the probability of cargo loss, enabling your team to take proactive action before losses occur.

## How it works

The prediction process follows a straightforward flow:

<Steps>
<Step title="Data arrives">
When new shipment data is loaded into the system, the prediction service is automatically triggered.
</Step>
<Step title="Shipments are analyzed">
The model analyzes each new CTRC using characteristics like route, cargo attributes, timing patterns, and historical data to produce a loss probability between 0 and 100%.
</Step>
<Step title="Risk levels are assigned">
Based on the probability, each CTRC is assigned a risk level:

| Risk Level | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **ALTO** (High) | Highest predicted probability of loss |
| **MEDIO** (Medium) | Moderate risk that warrants attention |
| **BAIXO** (Low) | Low probability of loss |

</Step>
<Step title="Results appear in the platform">
Predictions are immediately available in the platform's table view and collector, ready for your team to review and act on.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
The model only scores shipments that have not been scored yet, ensuring efficiency and preventing duplicate predictions.
</Callout>

## Understanding predictions

### Risk levels

Each prediction assigns one of three risk levels:

| Level | Badge Color | What it means |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| **ALTO** | Red | This CTRC has the highest predicted risk of cargo loss. Requires immediate attention and verification. |
| **MEDIO** | Yellow/Amber | Moderate risk. Should be monitored and verified when possible. |
| **BAIXO** | Green | Low risk. Routine handling is sufficient. |

### Probability

The probability is displayed as a percentage (e.g., 87.3%). A visual bar accompanies the number to give a quick sense of relative risk. The bar color matches the risk level badge.

### Confidence

The model also outputs a **confidence score** that reflects how certain the prediction is. Higher confidence means the characteristics of the shipment align strongly with known patterns.

## What the model considers

The prediction model analyzes multiple characteristics of each shipment, including:

- Route characteristics (origin, destination, distance)
- Cargo attributes (weight, value, type of goods)
- Timing patterns (day of week, month, time of year)
- Facility statistics (historical loss rates at origin and destination)
- Shipment details (number of volumes, freight value)

<Callout type="info">
The model is trained on historical shipment data where the outcome (loss or no loss) is known. It learns patterns that distinguish high-risk shipments from low-risk ones, then applies those patterns to score new shipments automatically.
</Callout>

## Recommended workflow

The recommended workflow for using Loss Prediction combines the Table View and Collector:

<Steps>
<Step title="Monitor the table">
Operations managers review the Loss Prediction table daily, sorted by probability (highest first), filtered to ALTO + MEDIO risk levels.
</Step>
<Step title="Identify high-risk shipments">
Look for clusters of high-risk CTRCs at specific units. This may indicate systematic issues at those facilities.
</Step>
<Step title="Dispatch field verification">
Share the unit code with field operators, who open the Collector on their mobile devices to verify high-risk cargo with photo evidence.
</Step>
<Step title="Export for reporting">
Use the CSV export feature to download prediction data for offline analysis, management reports, or integration with other systems.
</Step>
</Steps>
