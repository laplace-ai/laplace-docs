# Digital Twin — Evolution Roadmap

The platform provides a solid foundation that grows with your operation. At each stage, new services and decision-making layers are added on top of the same foundation — without rebuilding what has already been delivered.

---

| Stage | Name | Who acts | Primary value |
|-------|------|----------|---------------|
| 1 | **Digital Shadow** | Operator | Entire network in one place |
| 2 | **Scenario Simulation** | Operator, with system support | Decisions tested before execution |
| 3 | **Smart Recommendations** | System, with human approval | Suggestions with quantified impact |
| 4 | **Autopilot** | System, with human oversight | Optimized execution in real time |

---

## Stage 1 · Digital Shadow

### The system sees. The operator decides.

Today, logistics network information is scattered across TMS tables, spreadsheets, and daily operations. At this stage, the Digital Twin consolidates everything into a single real-time view.

The operator accesses a dashboard with the complete transfer network map: where each shipment is, the status of each leg, which deadlines are at risk. For any specific shipment, a detailed card can be opened with weight, value, origin, and history.

**What changes in operations:**
- Real-time unit occupancy and vehicle routes
- Visual representation of pickup and delivery points
- The entire logistics network represented in one place

---

## Stage 2 · Scenario Simulation

### The operator asks. The system answers.

With visibility resolved, the next step is allowing the operator to test alternatives before executing them.

In the first phase, comparison is direct — route A→C versus A→B→C — considering cost, transit time, and service level.

In the second phase, the system considers the operational context: *"if I send this shipment to hub B, will there be enough volume to consolidate tomorrow?"* or *"does the vehicle arriving at 2 PM have spare capacity?"* The system projects the future state of the network to provide more realistic answers.

**What changes in operations:**
- Route decisions are no longer based on intuition
- The operator justifies choices with numbers — "I simulated it and this option saves 12%"
- Planning errors decrease because the scenario was tested beforehand

---

## Stage 3 · Smart Recommendations

### The system suggests. The operator approves.

The initiative shifts sides. Instead of waiting for the operator's question, the system runs scenarios automatically and presents the best option already calculated.

Multiple strategies are evaluated in parallel. Results are compared — cost, deadline, vehicle occupancy — and delivered as ranked recommendations. The operator reviews, adjusts if necessary, and approves.

**What changes in operations:**
- The operator no longer needs to think "which scenarios should I test?" — the system has already tested them
- The team focuses on exceptions and strategic decisions
- Greater opportunity for global network optimization ROI

---

## Stage 4 · Autopilot

### The system flies solo. The operator takes the wheel only in turbulence.

The Digital Twin assumes execution of routine decisions. Cargo dispatch, route selection, volume consolidation — everything happens automatically, based on intelligence validated with the operation's real history.

The operator moves from executor to strategic supervisor: sets the rules, monitors indicators, and intervenes only when something goes off track.

**What changes in operations:**
- Operational decisions happen in seconds, not hours
- Intelligence continuously improves
- The human team focuses 100% on strategy, exceptions, and client relationships

---

*Evolution is incremental. Each stage delivers immediate value and serves as the foundation for the next.*
