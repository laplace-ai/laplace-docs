# Digital Twin

The Digital Twin is the heart of the Laplace Platform. It provides an interactive, map-based view of your entire logistics network — every facility, every route connection, all displayed on a real-time map.

This is the default landing page when you log into the platform.

## What you see on the map

The Digital Twin map displays three layers of information:

### Unit markers

Every logistics facility (warehouse, distribution center, hub, terminal, branch) in your network appears as a **blue marker** on the map. The markers are positioned at each facility's geographic location.

Hovering over a marker shows the unit code. Clicking a marker opens the **unit detail panel** on the right side of the screen.

### Route connections

Lines connecting unit markers represent the **network links** between facilities — the routes along which cargo flows. These connections help you visualize the shape and density of your logistics network.

When you select a unit, the connections to and from that unit are highlighted, making it easy to see that facility's place in the network.

### Map style

The map automatically adapts to your theme:

| Theme | Map Style |
|-------|-----------|
| Light mode | Clean, minimal with warm tones |
| Dark mode | Subdued, easy on the eyes |

## Interacting with the map

### Navigation

- **Zoom**: Scroll your mouse wheel to zoom in and out
- **Pan**: Click and drag to move around the map
- **Rotate**: Right-click and drag to rotate the map perspective

The map is centered on Brazil by default, as Laplace currently focuses on Brazilian logistics networks.

### Selecting a unit

Click any unit marker to open the **unit detail panel**. This panel slides in from the right side and shows:

- **Unit code** (e.g., "ATI SPA") with an active/inactive status indicator
- **Unit name** (e.g., "Sao Paulo")
- **Location** (city and state)
- **Unit type** (e.g., hub, branch, distribution center)
- **Status** (active or inactive)
- **Services section** (coming soon — will show loss prediction and analytics for the unit)
- A **"View Detailed Unit"** button that navigates to the full unit detail page

To close the panel, click the X button in the top-right corner of the panel, or click on the map background.

### Immersive mode

When the Digital Twin page is active, the map can enter **immersive mode** — hiding the top bar and bottom status bar to give you maximum screen space for the map. The sidebar remains visible for navigation.

This is useful when you want to present the network view on a large screen or projector.

## Route highlighting

When you click on a unit, the route connections change:

- **Connected routes** (routes to or from the selected unit) become more prominent
- **Other routes** fade to a subtle opacity

This visual highlighting helps you immediately understand which facilities are directly connected to the selected unit, revealing the unit's role in the logistics network.
